## SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM (one number, one story)

**Headline:** 500,000 ironworkers. 12–18 months to go signatory contractor.
$300–$500/hr consultants. Most give up.

**Body (three bullets, one line each):**
- Going from journeyman → signatory contractor = 7 phases across 50
  different regulatory regimes. Nobody teaches it.
- The info is scattered across Secretaries of State, 12 federal portals,
  200+ Ironworkers locals, and a handful of expensive attorneys.
- Result: qualified tradespeople either quit the process, stay on the
  tools, or drift non-union — hurting the trade, the unions, and the
  pipeline of real signatory contractors the industry needs.

**Pull-quote (right column, cyan):**
> "I've been trying to figure out how to go signatory for two years."
> — IW Local 29 welder, Portland

## SLIDE 3 — WHY NOW

Three converging forces:

1. **AI finally useful for regulatory navigation.** Claude + Retrieval
   grounding makes 50-state regulatory guidance reliable in a way ChatGPT
   alone is not.
2. **Federal infrastructure tailwind.** IIJA ($1.2T) + IRA ($369B clean
   energy) are mandating prevailing wage + project labor agreements,
   creating the biggest signatory-contractor demand spike in 40 years.
3. **Unions are organized but under-tooled.** IW International has made
   signatory contractor growth a stated org KPI; district councils are
   paying $80k+ staff to do this work with Google Docs and PDFs.

## APPENDIX C — RISKS & MITIGATIONS

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Regulatory liability (user follows wrong WC info, gets fined) | E&O insurance, per-page "last verified" timestamp, "not legal advice" disclaimer, quarterly refresh process |
| Stale state data | Quarterly audit process, automated SoS URL check script, bounty system for users flagging changes |
| Union political fallout (one council feuds with another) | Start with a single friendly council, let them evangelize, never position as "the" official tool of IW International until invited |
| Claude API dependency / cost | Streaming + prompt caching keeps cost < $0.10 per user-month at scale; abstract behind LLM router for optional GPT/Llama swap |
| Competitor enters the vertical | 9-month dataset head start + founder credibility + dual license |
| TAM ceiling on ironwork alone | Trade expansion framework — same 7-phase wizard reskinned for electrical, mechanical, plumbing |

## DECK DESIGN NOTES

- Use the product's cyberpunk aesthetic only on slides 1, 4, and 12. The
  rest: minimalist dark slides, JetBrains Mono for data, Space Grotesk for
  narrative. Avoid matrix-rain-everywhere fatigue.
- Every slide has at most one number you want the investor to remember.
- No slide of logos of companies you haven't sold to. Investors see
  through "inspired by" logo walls instantly.
- Final slide is the ask. Never end on "thank you" — end on the number.
