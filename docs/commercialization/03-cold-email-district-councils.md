# Cold Email Sequence — IW District Council Business Managers

> Audience: the 11 Ironworkers International District Council Business
> Managers (+ organizers, apprenticeship coordinators as warm CCs).
> Goal: book a 20-minute Zoom demo. NOT to close a contract cold.
>
> Posture: peer-to-peer, trade-to-trade. You are an ironworker who built a
> tool, not a SaaS salesman. Never lead with "revolutionary AI platform."
> Lead with a problem the BM already loses sleep over: **signatory
> contractor attrition**.
>
> Cadence: 4-touch sequence over 18 days. Stop the moment they reply.

---

## PRE-SEND CHECKLIST

- [ ] Sender identity matches what the BM can verify: personal email with
      `firstname@ironforge.app`, email signature lists your local #, years
      in the trade, and a link to your LinkedIn showing welding photos —
      not a headshot in a polo.
- [ ] Research the council: name the district, the general VP, any recent
      press (new signatory contractor, strike, apprenticeship expansion,
      Davis-Bacon news in their jurisdiction). First line must prove you
      did homework.
- [ ] Dry-run link: send yourself a test and verify the Calendly loads on
      mobile. Most BMs open email on a phone in a truck.
- [ ] Attach nothing. First email = text only. Attachments = spam filter.

## WHAT TO DO WHEN THEY REPLY

### If they say "send more info":
DO NOT send a PDF deck. Reply with ONE paragraph and a Calendly link.
Your conversion rate on "deck attached" is zero. BMs want to hear you.

### If they say "not interested":
Reply once: "Understood — thanks for taking the time to reply. If you
ever want to revisit, my line's open. In solidarity, [Name]." Then
remove from the sequence. Do NOT argue.

### If they say "talk to [other person]":
Immediate warm intro email, cc'ing the BM, same day. Subject: `Intro
per [BM First Name] — IronForge + [District Council Name]`.

### If they book the demo:
- Send a 2-line confirmation with the Zoom link (not Calendly's
  auto-email — a human one).
- 24 hours before: send a 1-line reminder + the per-council personalized
  URL so they can click around in advance.
- Show up 2 minutes early. Wear a union t-shirt, not a Patagonia vest.

## DEMO AGENDA (20 min, leave 5 for Q&A)

1. **(2 min) Why I built this.** One-paragraph story. Your local.
   A member you watched fail to go signatory. The $400/hr lawyer quote.
2. **(6 min) Walk their district.** Click into their locals. Show hall
   info, trust fund breakdown, CBA walkthrough, bond requirements for
   a state in their jurisdiction.
3. **(4 min) Walk a member's journey.** Pick a hypothetical Local [##]
   journeyman. Pick the state. Walk LLC → license → bond → signatory in
   fast-forward.
4. **(3 min) The AI mentor.** Ask Claude a question about their
   jurisdiction live. Show it refuses out-of-scope questions (builds
   trust that it won't hallucinate at a member).
5. **(3 min) The partnership model.** Co-branded deployment. White-label
   as "[District Council] Signatory Launchpad powered by IronForge."
   Pricing: $25k–$75k/yr depending on council size, member seats
   included. Offer a 90-day pilot at $0 if they'll commit a point of
   contact and 3 members for case studies.
6. **(2 min + Q&A) The ask.** "Will you run a 90-day pilot with 3
   members from [locals] and a point of contact from your staff?"

## DO / DON'T

**DO**
- Write like you're texting a fellow BA. Contractions. Trade nouns.
- Name your local and your years in the trade in every first touch.
- Personalize the jurisdiction detail in the second touch.
- Offer a free 90-day pilot. Paid enterprise deals follow free pilots
  that delivered a member success story.

**DON'T**
- Use words like "revolutionize," "disrupt," "game-changing," "ecosystem."
  These are instant-delete words at a union hall.
- Send a PDF one-pager on first touch.
- Mention valuation, fundraising, or "Silicon Valley" anywhere. You are
  a welder who built software, not a founder burning VC money.
- CC the General President of IW International. Work the councils first.
  HQ sales motions come after you have 3 signed councils.
